## What Is a Bushfire Attack Level (BAL)?

The Bushfire Attack Level is a standardised measure of a building's potential exposure to bushfire attack. It's assessed under Australian Standard AS 3959-2018 and considers factors including vegetation type near the property, slope of the land, and distance from bushland. The BAL determines what construction standards apply if you build or renovate.

## How Bushfire Risk Affects You

### Insurance

This is where bushfire risk hits your wallet hardest. Properties in BAL-29 and above can face insurance premiums hundreds to thousands of dollars higher per year than equivalent properties in BAL-LOW areas. At the extreme end, some insurers refuse to cover properties rated BAL-FZ (Flame Zone) at all. If you're buying, call your insurer with the property address before you commit â€” find out what the premium will be. An extra $2,000/year in insurance over a 30-year mortgage is $60,000.

### Building and renovations

If you plan to build or renovate on a property within the BMO, you'll need to comply with bushfire construction standards (AS 3959). This adds cost: bushfire-rated windows, non-combustible cladding, ember guards, and in some cases, specific landscaping requirements within the "defendable space" around the home. For new builds, the additional bushfire compliance cost can range from $20,000 to $80,000+ depending on the BAL rating.

### Property value

Bushfire risk is increasingly factored into property valuations. Research by the University of Melbourne found that properties in high-risk bushfire areas sell for 5-10% less than comparable properties outside these zones, and this discount has been growing since 2019-2020. Buyers are more aware, insurers are more cautious, and lenders are starting to factor climate risk into lending decisions.

## What You Can Do to Reduce Risk

If you already own in a bushfire-prone area, there are practical steps to improve your safety and potentially reduce insurance costs: maintain defensible space around your home (clear vegetation within 10-30 metres), ensure gutters and roof spaces are clear of leaves and debris, install ember guards on vents and gaps, keep a bushfire survival plan and ensure all household members know it, and consider progressive upgrades to bushfire-rated materials when you renovate.

The CFA (Country Fire Authority) offers free property assessments in high-risk areas â€” [cfa.vic.gov.au](https://www.cfa.vic.gov.au) has details on how to request one.
